What is Vendor Risk Assessment?

Under GDPR Article 28, any organisation (controller) that uses a third-party service (processor) to handle personal data must verify that processor meets GDPR standards before sharing any data. This is called processor due diligence.

You are reviewing 6 real processors used by HealthBridge — from cloud infrastructure (AWS) to payroll (ADP) to marketing (HubSpot). For each, you must check: Does a binding DPA exist? Is data transferred outside the EU? Are SCCs and TIAs in place? Then make a compliance determination.

Getting this wrong in the real world means regulatory fines up to €20M / 4% annual turnover (Art. 83(5)) — and direct liability for the controller, not the processor.

What to Look For in Each Assessment
DPA Existence Is there a signed, binding Data Processing Agreement under Art. 28? Without one, all processing by this vendor is unlawful.
Hosting & Transfers Where is data stored? US hosting requires Chapter V mechanisms. EU-only hosting avoids transfer obligations entirely.
SCCs & TIA Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2 = C2P) plus a Transfer Impact Assessment are mandatory for non-EU transfers post Schrems II.

What is a RoPA? — Article 30, GDPR
A Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) is a mandatory internal register under GDPR Article 30. Every organisation processing personal data must maintain it. It documents what data you process, why (lawful basis), who the data subjects are, how long you keep it, and which vendors are involved. Regulators expect it to be current, accurate, and demonstrably maintained. Your workshop answers auto-generate each entry. Click any row to see a full compliance breakdown.

Data Mapping Walkthrough: Analyst Guide

1. Walkthrough Overview

Welcome to the HealthBridge data mapping walkthrough. As a Privacy Analyst, your objective is to review operational processing activities, identify GDPR compliance gaps, and formally map data flows into the Record of Processing Activities (RoPA).

2. Data Discovery Workshop

The core of your workflow lies in the Data Mapping Workshop tab. Here, you will read through stakeholder interview transcripts and extract regulatory metadata.

  • Identify the correct Primary Lawful Basis (Art. 6).
  • Flag Special Category Data (Art. 9) and apply heightened protections (Explicit Consent / Exemption).
  • Enforce Storage Limitation (Art. 5) by correcting infinite retention periods.

3. Vendor Risk Sandbox

Third-party SaaS processors represent a significant supply-chain risk. In the Vendor Sandbox, you will conduct Due Diligence on our subprocessors.

  • Ensure all vendors have a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) per Article 28.
  • If a vendor transfers data to the US, you must certify a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) and select the correct Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) Module (Typically Module 2: Controller-to-Processor).
  • Activities mapped to a high-risk vendor are flagged so you can remediate the vendor in the Vendor Worksheet.

4. Guided vs. Independent Mode

You can toggle modes in the top navigation. Guided Mode shows reference answers and explanations as you go. Independent Mode hides them — try the scenarios cold first.

Goal: Map all 8 processing activities accurately and flag the compliance gaps in each one. This is a personal learning exercise — there's no score.
